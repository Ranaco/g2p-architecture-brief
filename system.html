Government-to-Person Disbursement · System One-Pager

Every public dollar, signed by an independent institution — and impossible for any one of them to forge.

A permissioned blockchain records the full custody of a government payment, from treasury to citizen. The money never leaves the banking rails; the chain holds only tamper-proof proof. No single institution — not even the one running the program — can rewrite the record.

Non-custodial · records, not money 4 institutions · Besu QBFT Chain off the critical path
What it does — and doesn’t — prove

An honest claim

✓ It proves
  • Every step is attributable to a specific institution’s key.
  • No record can be altered or erased without 3 of 4 independent bodies colluding.
  • Each bank’s settlement of each payout (by UTR) is signed and non-repudiable.
✕ It does not
  • Catch ghost or duplicate citizens — that is beaten upstream by national ID / biometrics.
  • Prove cash reached a hand — only that funds were settled to the account.
  • Move money — banks do that, on existing rails.
Architecture · one picture

Two lanes: money on rails, proof on the chain

The two layers are deliberately separate: a payment succeeds on a normal bank settlement; the chain is a witness that records who signed what.

Why no one can cheat

Trust comes from 3-of-4, not from a promise

Each institution runs one validator and signs with its own key. A block — a record — only becomes final when 3 of the 4 independent institutions cryptographically agree. The Ministry cannot insert a fake payment or delete a real one; it is one vote of four.

FINCBMINAUD
3 of 4 agree → record is final & irreversible. The network survives 1 malicious or offline institution (QBFT: N ≥ 3f+1). Onboarding a 5th institution is itself an on-chain vote.

The independent seat is the national audit court — constitutionally independent of the executive, so easy for a government to accept. It always runs a validator (so no record can be rewritten without it), but it actively signs only the final reconciliation — the one moment the system certifies the money reached the right people. Civil-society / NGO observers get read-only access and may file an on-chain dispute at any step — permanent, un-buriable — but carry no signing burden and cannot freeze payments.

The last mile · how “paid” is proven

The bank’s signed receipt is the proof

On send, each of the 10,000 payouts gets a unique endToEndId. The paying bank disburses, then returns a cryptographically signed callback carrying the settlement reference (UTR). The system auto-matches it. Only signed, matched receipts count as paid — everything else is treated as unpaid and flagged.

Ministry → 10,000 items, each with endToEndId        // docHash anchored on-chain
      ↓
Bank pays, signs & POSTs { endToEndId, utr, status:"PAID" }   // signature = the attestation
      ↓  auto-match endToEndId + amount + account
   ├─ matched + signed   →  PAID    (UTR recorded · outcome in Merkle root)
   └─ missing / mismatch →  NOT PAID · FLAGGED for investigation

Non-negotiable

  • The callback is signed with the bank’s key — a plain POST is forgeable, and that is exactly where fraud would re-enter.
  • The signature makes the bank unable to later deny it paid.

Scale, kept cheap

  • Outcomes are batched into a Merkle root anchored on-chain — not 10,000 writes.
  • Any single citizen’s payment is provable with a small inclusion proof.
On the chain · deliberately almost nothing

Proofs, never data

Per record, on-chain: { batchId, itemId, status, docHash, HMAC(citizenId), signer } plus batch Merkle roots. Never names, IDs in the clear, account numbers, or amounts — those stay in an encrypted, erasable database (delete the salt to erase). Each night the ledger’s root is anchored to Bitcoin, so not even the four institutions together can back-date history.
